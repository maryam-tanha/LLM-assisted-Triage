LLM-Assisted Triage in Acute Care Settings

Dr. E. Thorne1, M. Chen2, Dr. S. Patel1,3

1Dept of Emergency Medicine, General Hospital | 2AI Research Lab, Tech Univ | 3Institute for Medical Informatics

Background

Emergency Departments (EDs) face unprecedented overcrowding, leading to delayed triage and potentially adverse patient outcomes. Traditional triage relies heavily on rapid human assessment, which is prone to cognitive fatigue.

  • Current triage protocols (e.g., ESI) show high inter-rater variability.
  • Average wait times for initial assessment exceed recommended benchmarks by 40% in urban centers.
  • Retrospective studies indicate 12-15% of high-acuity cases are initially under-triaged.
[Figure 1: ED Volume vs Triage Delay]

Motivations

Recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) present an opportunity to process unstructured clinical narratives (triage notes, historical EHR data) rapidly.

  • Goal: Develop a low-latency pipeline to augment, not replace, nursing triage.
  • Requirement: Maintain strict data privacy (HIPAA compliance) via local deployment.
  • Challenge: Mitigate model hallucination in high-stakes clinical decision making.

Pipeline Architecture

The proposed system architecture routes incoming textual and tabular patient data through a localized, quantized 70B parameter model, specifically fine-tuned on medical corpora.

Targets

Primary operational and clinical targets for the deployed system:

  1. Latency: Processing time under 2.5 seconds per patient record.
  2. Sensitivity: >98% sensitivity for ESI Levels 1 & 2 (High Acuity) to ensure safe fail-overs.
  3. Explainability: System must output a concise rationale (max 50 words) referencing specific clinical guidelines.

Validation

We conducted a retrospective validation using a stratified sample of 15,000 anonymized ED encounters from Jan-Dec 2023.

  • Gold Standard: Consensus of 3 senior triage nurses.
  • Metrics computed: F1-Score, AUROC, Cohen's Kappa for inter-rater reliability.

Conclusions

LLM integration into the acute care triage process is technically feasible and shows promise in reducing cognitive load on clinical staff.

While the system exhibits high sensitivity for critical conditions, further prospective clinical trials are required to assess the impact of integration on actual patient outcomes and overall ED flow.

Future Work:

Integration with real-time vitals monitoring telemetry and multi-modal input processing.
