LLM-ASSISTED TRIAGE IN ACUTE CARE SETTINGS

Authors: Dr. E. Ripley, M. Bishop, A. Ash · Institute of Advanced Clinical AI

science Background

Acute care environments demand rapid, high-accuracy triage decisions under conditions of extreme time pressure and incomplete patient data.

Traditional heuristics often fail to synthesize multimodal inputs (vitals, free-text nursing notes, historical EMR data) simultaneously.

  • Emergency Department overcrowding delays initial assessment.
  • Cognitive load on triage nurses leads to misclassification of acuity.
  • Need for a scalable, deterministic processing pipeline that augments human decision-making without replacing clinical judgment.

target Motivations

We hypothesize that a fine-tuned Large Language Model integrated into the clinical workflow can standardise triage scoring (e.g., ESI - Emergency Severity Index) by continuously parsing streaming patient data.

> Objective_01: Reduce Time-to-Triage (TTT) by 40%
> Objective_02: Increase inter-rater reliability among triage staff
> Objective_03: Identify cryptic deterioration signals early

analytics Key Results

The fine-tuned model outperformed baseline clinician triage on accuracy while matching human sensitivity for critical (ESI 1 & 2) cases.

Processing text notes significantly improved classification of borderline cases compared to vital-signs-only models.

lightbulb Conclusions

LLM-assisted triage is viable under strict latency and accuracy constraints when using quantized, domain-adapted models.

Future work will focus on multirater human-in-the-loop deployment to measure trust and workflow integration friction.
